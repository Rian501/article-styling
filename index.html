Day Five Reflections

Today I am back at school. I feel more behind than ever before! But I am trying to go easy on the frustration with that. My brain feels like it is actually moving slower than I am used to, and it takes me longer to remember things, and I am more forgetful. I am blaming this on the concussion and hoping it is temporary, so that I can get back to being the self I remember. But, I am also trying to remind myself to be patient, especially now, because being impatient is only going to make me stressed out, and is not actually going to help me work any faster. Still, it's counterintuitive to me to catch up by taking it slow, to advance by resting.

This week has been pretty rough. I'm enjoying the group project, but am a bit anxious about all the work I feel behind in. I know I have to give myself a break and go at a reasonable pace, even to slow it down more than I would typically think of as a reasonable pace, but it is my usual way to prefer trying to race ahead.

Today I was able to make a nav bar for our group product page, and our group was also able to navigate (apparently the hard way of) resolving changes with merging branches in github. A week ago, although I had a github account and had gone through the small tutorial of branching and merging a branch with myself, I really had no idea what github was or how it worked. Now I have some idea. I had also never made an HTML page from scratch, even an ugly one, nor had I made a nav bar, even a somewhat clunky one. I had watched some Treehouse videos, but I had never actually made anything on my own or with others from a blank file. So, while I still feel lost and behind, there's been progress this week.

I will try to do some work over the weekend, but also to rest, and deal with things like insurance and other headaches that come from the same source as, though not only directly from, the concussion.

Emily Lemmon

5/26/2017

It is now Friday again, and I am feeling a little more confident, a little less lost, and a lot happier. I freaking hate my life outside of school right now (dealing with insurance companies, not having transportation, etc.), but within the school walls and hours I find that I really enjoy learning all this new stuff and trying to make it work. Every bug fix feels like a revelation and a triumph.

I used to stress about school, and have anxiety about being behind. I felt that way last week, too, because the exercise list coupled with my remaining pre-work basically convinced me that I was a loser and potentially irredeemable as far as hoping to make enough progress to 'get there' by the end of the course. I feel a lot calmer about it now. I think the constant emphasis on growth over achievement is helping.
